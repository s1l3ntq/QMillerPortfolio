About

My name is Q’Sean Miller, I’m bilingual – but not in the conventional sense of the word. I’m the youngest of my mother’s two children – and my big sister was born deaf. Because of this - I learned how to speak English and American sign language simultaneously – fluent in both at a young age. The skill came with some perks but also a lot of responsibility. Despite being the little brother, by default, my ability to bridge the gap between my sister and the hearing world also made me her protector, her voice, and her interpreter. Her challenges became mine and exposed me first hand to the barriers in accessibility she and others in the deaf community face. It’s something that lingers in the back of mind as I embark on this journey to becoming a software engineer. As technology evolves – and we find ourselves relying on it more and more – we need to make sure that we’re not leaving anyone behind. One way to do that is factoring accessibility and inclusion into the infancy of software development instead of as an afterthought.

I am a graduate of Flatiron School where I received my certificate in Software Engineering. I have deployed applications to a production environment using automated software change pipelines. I have worked in a full agile software development process including mining technical business features and creating user stories to accomplish business goals. I also read, wrote, and reviewed application code, with a focus on writing maintainable code that is easy to understand.

I am seeking a position that will aid me in gaining experience using various skills listed in my resume to gain the necessary knowledge to apply to the company. I am no stranger to hard work. I have exceptional communication skills, thrive in fast paced environments where I am able to maintain multiple tasks, self-motivated to be able to work independently, and I am very capable to work in a team-oriented setting.

- Q'Sean Miller
